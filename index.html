Spotlighting local pets and the people who love them

This article features different pets each time, focusing on the pets first, then a bit about their people, and how they are connected in and around our mountain communities.

You will also find pet related topics, such as breed-specific information, and relevant ways that you can get involved in helping others (both animals and people), in and around our area. There will always be diversity in the breed and background of the pets, from rescues to purebreds, and everything in between. I hope you feel inspired, learn something new, or simply enjoy the stories. So please read on…

This week’s story holds a bit of urgency. It is about two cats,  one of which, unfortunately, has recently gone missing.

Let’s start with the first who is eagerly awaiting his brother’s return.

Meet Filou Orangesaurus!

Filou is a 14 year old orange tabby - hence the playful nickname. Filou is French for ‘thief’, so named because - “He could learn to unlock anything that didn’t lock with more torque than his body could manage. Filou is unusually intelligent for a cat. His first trip to the vet he escaped their locked kennel 4 times.”

He came to his owner back in Lansing Michigan when they were awakened by a banging on the window. It was a small orange kitten that had adult sized scratches across its nose.

His owner continues: “Filou is incredibly loyal but shy since his surgery. He had congenital necrosis of the femoral head as a young cat. This manifested as a broken hip and required surgery on each side to remove it. In addition to costing as much as a car, the first surgery almost killed him, and he has been very scared of strangers ever since. In his own way he is one of the most courageous and loyal cats I’ve ever known. He considers it his job to make sure everyone in the house is safe. If my wife and I disagree, he’ll insert himself in the middle and push us away from each other.”

Filou is too smart for his own good as he can unlock things with ease that cats should not be able to. His owner has video of him systematically testing how to open every door and cupboard in their kitchen!

Next, meet Loki Doodle!

Loki is an 11 year old black short haired cat. He was originally found wandering a golf course in Michigan, and was adopted at 6 months old as company for Filou while his ‘fur Dad’ spent long days at work. Loki is quite clumsy, getting  himself pinned behind the refrigerator, or television. Many times, his ‘brother’ Filou would poke Dad in the face in the middle of the night and lead him to where Loki was stuck. He has also been known to fall off of a chair where he sat, for no reason at all.

He was named Loki after the Norse trickster god. His owner lovingly tells us:

“What he lacks in grace Loki makes up for in heart. He’s the most friendly cat you’ll ever meet. We used to allow him to have limited adventures wearing a gps collar when we lived in enclosed communities. Quite literally, everyone who met him became friends with Loki. Everywhere we lived he became legendary as the friendliest cat.”

Loki loves food, but if not allowed to eat something, will sit with it, resulting in him ultimately being given a small piece. His owner says: “In our house, small bits of food are always called ‘Loki pieces’. He is both incredibly caring and patient. He has slept next to my son almost since Augustine was born – despite the fact that Auggie is still too young to understand that it is not ok to pull the kittie’s tail. Loki is caring and sensitive, making us laugh in our saddest moments. He’s also a dutiful brother, and, despite his crazed love of food, he always insists on letting Filou eat his fill first. He loves to lie on his back with his legs straight up in the air. More than once I was sure he was dead before I learned he just likes to sleep like that. He also loves to jump on people’s backs when they bend over. Dozens of times when I didn’t even know he was in the room, I would bend over to pick something up, only to have the wind knocked out of me as Loki jumped onto my back.”

Loki went missing in the early morning of March 20th, 2025. After a difficult, long week of work, his ‘fur Dad’ had left the door ajar after falling asleep early. He was awakened in the middle of the night to discover the door was open, and Loki was gone. Of course, he feels terrible but it was an honest mistake, and as we all know, cats are curious. They spent the entire night searching, and have not stopped since.

So, who are Loki and Filou’s people?

Meet Marc and Vivienne Bittner!

Marc moved from Ann Arbor Michigan to Los Angeles in 2016, bringing both cats with him. For nearly 20 years, Marc has worked in technology in senior and leadership positions with Lifetime Fitness, Ring, Amazon and others. He is currently head of engineering for Chatterworks, an AI startup company that focuses on building recruiting software platforms.

He and his wife Vivienne moved to Lake Arrowhead about a year and a half ago, as it has always been one of their favorite vacation spots. Their home is shared with their son Augustine “Boo Boo”. Vivienne moved from China for business school in 2008. She’s worked in a number of design focused capacities at design agencies and tech companies, in such positions as Vice President of Design and Head of Product. She is a dedicated Mom, first to the kitties and now to their 2 year old son.

Vivienne especially appreciates outdoor activities such as hiking, and they both love the natural beauty our mountains have to offer.

Marc shares with exuberance:

“I am specifically fond of the overall goodness of people here and the character of the mountain community. One of the things we’ve been very grateful for is how helpful almost everyone we’ve met has been. Truly, it has moved our hearts with how many people are out looking for Loki. We’ve had dozens of reports and I’ve run into little children holding his flyer trying to find him. Unfortunately, there are many all black cats in this area, and if Loki survived, he’d have gotten somewhat away from our house. We are continuing to track down leads and are offering a reward. We intend to give $500 to anyone who calls us with any information that results in his return, and if someone caught him and turned him over, we intend to pay $1000 no questions asked. Alternatively we can donate that money to a charity of the person’s choice if they prefer.”

Loki has three traits to help identify him.

*A white spot on his belly

*A larger, almost swollen looking belly

* An unusually short and pointy tail.

In closing, many of us know that gut-wrenching feeling of losing a beloved pet. So please keep an eye out for this special cat.

From Marc - “We pray everyday that Loki can be found unharmed. We love him to an extent that is almost absurd and hard to explain. He’s only a cat, but he’s the best of us. We’ve been lucky to have eleven years with such a remarkable animal, and we will go to any length to bring him back home. Filou spends many nights meowing at the door, as if to call out to Loki to come home.”

Patrice Mock has been a Lake Arrowhead resident for over 25 years, is a life-long animal welfare advocate, works at our local middle school, in animal rescue, and is a part time pet sitter.
